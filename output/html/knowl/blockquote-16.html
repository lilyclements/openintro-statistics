Poisson distribution
Suppose we are watching for events and the number of observed events follows a Poisson distribution with rate \(\lambda\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} P(\text{observe $k$ events}) = \frac{\lambda^{k} e^{-\lambda}}{k!} \end{equation*}
where \(k\) may take a value 0, 1, 2, and so on, and \(k!\) represents \(k\)-factorial, as described on page SectionÂ 4.3. The letter \(e \approx 2.718\) is the base of the natural logarithm. The mean and standard deviation of this distribution are \(\lambda\) and \(\sqrt{\lambda}\text{,}\) respectively.
