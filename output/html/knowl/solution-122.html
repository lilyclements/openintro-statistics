Solution 4.3.4.1.

We are asked to determine the expected number (the mean) and the standard deviation, both of which can be directly computed from the formulas: \(\mu = np = 40 \times 0.7 = 28\) and \(\sigma = \sqrt{np(1-p)} = \sqrt{40 \times 0.7 \times 0.3} = 2.9\text{.}\) Because very roughly 95% of observations fall within 2 standard deviations of the mean (see Section 1.6.3), we would probably observe at least 22 but fewer than 34 individuals in our sample who would not exceed the deductible.
