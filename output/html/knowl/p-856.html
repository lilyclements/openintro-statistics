Suppose the probability of a single trial being a success is \(p\text{.}\) Then the probability of observing exactly \(k\) successes in \(n\) independent trials is given by
\begin{equation*} \binom{n}{k}p^k(1-p)^{n-k} = \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}p^k(1-p)^{n-k} \end{equation*}
The mean, variance, and standard deviation of the number of observed successes are
\begin{align*} \mu \amp = np \amp \sigma^2 \amp = np(1-p) \amp \sigma \amp = \sqrt{np(1-p)} \end{align*}
