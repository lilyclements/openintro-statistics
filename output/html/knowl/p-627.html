The expression for John’s commute time was
\begin{equation*} X_1 + X_2 + X_3 + X_4 + X_5 \end{equation*}
Each coefficient is 1, and the variance of each day’s time is \(4^2 = 16\text{.}\) Thus, the variance of the total weekly commute time is
\begin{align*} \text{variance} \amp= 1^2 \times 16 + 1^2 \times 16 + 1^2 \times 16 + 1^2 \times 16 + 1^2 \times 16 = 5 \times 16 = 80\\ \text{standard deviation} \amp= \sqrt{\text{variance}} = \sqrt{80} = 8.94 \end{align*}
The standard deviation for John’s weekly work commute time is about 9 minutes.
