Suppose we wanted to compute the probability of observing 49, 50, or 51 smokers in 400 when \(p=0.15\text{.}\) With such a large sample, we might be tempted to apply the normal approximation and use the range 49 to 51. However, we would find that the binomial solution and the normal approximation notably differ:
\begin{align*} \text{Binomial:} \amp \text{ } 0.0649 \amp \text{Normal:} \amp \text{ } 0.0421 \end{align*}
