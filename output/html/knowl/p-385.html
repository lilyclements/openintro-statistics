Data scientists rarely work with individual outcomes and instead consider sets or collections of outcomes. Let \(A\) represent the event where a die roll results in 1 or 2 and \(B\) represent the event that the die roll is a 4 or a 6. We write \(A\) as the set of outcomes \(\\{1, 2\\}\) and \(B=\\{4, 6\\}\text{.}\) These sets are commonly called events.
