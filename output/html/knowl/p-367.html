Probability can be illustrated by rolling a die many times. Let \(\\hat{p}_n\) be the proportion of outcomes that are 1 after the first \(n\) rolls. As the number of rolls increases, \(\\hat{p}_n\) will converge to the probability of rolling a 1, \(p = 1/6\text{.}\) FigureÂ 3.1.6 shows this convergence for 100,000 die rolls. The tendency of \(\\hat{p}_n\) to stabilize around \(p\) is described by the Law of Large Numbers.
