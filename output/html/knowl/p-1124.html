When the parameter is a proportion, it is often denoted by \(p\text{,}\) and we often refer to the sample proportion as \(\hat{p}\) (pronounced p-hat). Unless we collect responses from every individual in the population, \(p\) remains unknown, and we use \(\hat{p}\) as our estimate of \(p\text{.}\) The difference we observe from the poll versus the parameter is called the error in the estimate.
