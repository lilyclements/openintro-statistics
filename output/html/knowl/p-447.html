Suppose the variables handedness and sex are independent, i.e. knowing someone’s sex provides no useful information about their handedness and vice-versa. Then we can compute whether a randomly selected person is right-handed and female 2  using the Multiplication Rule:
\begin{align*} P(\text{right-handed and female}) \amp= P(\text{right-handed}) \times P(\text{female})\\ \amp= 0.91 \times 0.50 = 0.455 \end{align*}
