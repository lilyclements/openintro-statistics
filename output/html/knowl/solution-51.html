Solution 3.2.15.1.

We will compute our answer using the General Multiplication Rule and then verify it using Figure 3.2.11. We want to determine \(P(\text{result} = \text{lived and inoculated} = \text{no})\) and we are given that \(P(\text{result} = \text{lived} \mid \text{inoculated} = \text{no}) = 0.8588\) and \(P(\text{inoculated} = \text{no}) = 0.9608\text{.}\)
Among the 96.08% of people who were not inoculated, 85.88% survived:
\begin{equation*} P(\text{result} = \text{lived and inoculated} = \text{no}) = 0.8588 \times 0.9608 = 0.8251 \end{equation*}
This is equivalent to the General Multiplication Rule. We can confirm this probability in Figure 3.2.11 at the intersection of “no” and “lived” (with a small rounding error).
