The histogram in Figure 4.5.2 approximates a Poisson distribution with rate equal to 4.4. The rate for a Poisson distribution is the average number of occurrences in a mostly-fixed population per unit of time. In Example 4.5.1, the time unit is a day, the population is all New York City residents, and the historical rate is 4.4. The parameter in the Poisson distribution is the rate—or how many events we expect to observe—and it is typically denoted by \(\lambda\) (the Greek letter lambda) or \(\mu\text{.}\) Using the rate, we can describe the probability of observing exactly \(k\) events in a single unit of time.
