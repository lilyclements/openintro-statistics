The scenario outlined in Example 4.3.1 is an example of a binomial distribution scenario. The binomial distribution describes the probability of having exactly \(k\) successes in \(n\) independent Bernoulli trials with probability of a success \(p\) (in Example 4.3.1, \(n=4\text{,}\) \(k=3\text{,}\) \(p=0.7\)). We would like to determine the probabilities associated with the binomial distribution more generally, i.e. we want a formula where we can use \(n\text{,}\) \(k\text{,}\) and \(p\) to obtain the probability. To do this, we reexamine each part of Example 4.3.1.
