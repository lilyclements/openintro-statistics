Example 5.2.3. 95% CI for solar energy support.

In a Pew Research poll, 887 out of 1000 American adults (88.7%) supported expanding the use of solar energy. Compute and interpret a 95% confidence interval for the population proportion.
ðŸ”—
Solution.
First, we verify the conditions. The observations are from a random sample, so they are independent. We check the success-failure condition:
\begin{align*} n\hat{p} \amp= 1000 \times 0.887 = 887 \geq 10\\ n(1-\hat{p}) \amp= 1000 \times 0.113 = 113 \geq 10 \end{align*}
Both are greater than 10, so the conditions are met.
ðŸ”—
The standard error is:
\begin{align*} SE_{\hat{p}} \amp= \sqrt{\frac{0.887 \times 0.113}{1000}}\\ \amp= 0.0101 \end{align*}
ðŸ”—
The 95% confidence interval is:
\begin{align*} 0.887 \pm 1.96 \times 0.0101 \amp= 0.887 \pm 0.0198\\ \amp= (0.8672, 0.9068) \end{align*}
ðŸ”—
Interpretation: We are 95% confident that between 86.7% and 90.7% of American adults support expanding the use of solar energy.
